# Map Canon Rules

## Prime Rule

The markdown notes define canon. The generated map images define visual direction.

If image text is unclear, misspelled, too small to read, or introduces labels not listed in canon notes, treat it as decorative or non-canon until approved.

## World Scale

Aereth is larger than the currently known mapped lands. The previous six-continent map remains canon only as the **First Known Map** or known-world prototype, not the full planet/world.

The current map hierarchy presents:

1. The known world.
2. The fogbound unknown around it.
3. Major metaphysical boundaries.
4. Player progression direction.
5. Political and cultural influence zones.

## Known Macro-Divisions

- **West**: early game, continuity, trade, records, recovery, starter routes.
- **North**: midgame, endurance, survival, remembrance, harsh clans, memory-loss culture.
- **East**: advanced midgame, doctrine, forbidden knowledge, archives, mutation theories, fire-forged belief.
- **Center**: late game, unstable reality, relic danger, the Fractured Expanse.
- **South**: endgame/finality, Void Seas descent, Absolute Void.

## Known Continents

- **Valterra**: western/northwestern known continent; anchored, settled, record-linked.
- **Lyssara**: western/southwestern known continent; coast, trade, old ports, frontier routes.
- **Kaer Morr**: northern known continent; frost, clans, grave-memory, endurance.
- **Nox Aetern**: northeast/eastern known continent; archives, night, doctrine, forbidden knowledge.
- **Solmyr**: east/southeast known continent; fire, law, red belief, desert/cathedral imagery.
- **Elderwyth**: southeast/east-south frontier; living memory, wild growth, old nature.

## Major World Features

- **The Fractured Expanse** sits in the Center and must remain visually dominant.
- **Void Seas** surround dangerous outer routes and unstable travel boundaries.
- **The Absolute Void** sits in the far South as a final/endgame boundary.

## Fog Rules

Fog exists for different reasons. It is not just weather.

- **Cartographic Fog**: no reliable surveys.
- **Erasure Fog**: reality or memory damage.
- **Political Fog**: conflicting faction claims or suppressed records.
- **Mythic Fog**: folklore, impossible geography, living legends.
- **Void Fog**: proximity to void contamination.

Future continents may exist in West, North, East, and Center outer areas. South is **not** normal expansion space; it is finality/endgame space.

## Map Images Are In-World Artifacts

The maps can be treated as different in-world documents:

- Macro map: broad mystical atlas.
- Detailed atlas: scholar/cartographer known-world view.
- Progression map: player-facing adventurer route guide.
- Political/cultural map: contested faction atlas with marginalia.

No single map is assumed perfectly objective.
