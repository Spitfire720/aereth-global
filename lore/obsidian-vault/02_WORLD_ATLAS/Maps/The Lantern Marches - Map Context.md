# The Lantern Marches - Map Context

## Canon Position

The Lantern Marches are the starter subregion of Aereth.

They sit in the western world between Valterra and Lyssara influence, close enough to Hallowmere-style memory weirdness to feel strange, but stable enough to support early play.

## Map Role

On maps, The Lantern Marches must appear as a **frontier route-region**, not a continent.

They should look like:

- a marchland
- a corridor
- a broken road network
- a buffer region
- a starter frontier
- a place between stronger cultural zones

## Required Ingredients

- stable settlement
- small archive or registry post
- broken road network
- reflection/memory anomaly
- abandoned outpost or shrine
- low-risk Fragment disturbance site
- outward route into wider West
- visible sign that the world beyond is larger and more dangerous

## Main Hub

Primary starter hub:

- Lantern's Rest

## Suggested Internal Places

- The Bent Road
- Mirrorfen
- The Ash Mile
- Saint's Crossing
- Old Wick Post
- The Drowned Mile
- The Quiet Ledger
- Hollowglass Pool

## Visual Direction

The Lantern Marches should visually combine:

- warm lantern light
- broken roads
- wetlands or reflective pools
- ruined milestones
- archive markers
- small watchposts
- abandoned roadside shrines
- mist and memory distortion

It should feel safer than the wider world, but not peaceful. Aereth should never feel like a tutorial island where nothing has gone wrong. This world has manners, unfortunately, but not mercy.

## Gameplay Direction

The Lantern Marches should support:

- first race/world introduction
- first Fragment disturbance
- first registry/archive interaction
- first profession hints
- first road/route decisions
- first glimpse of Hallowmere weirdness
- first reason to travel outward

## Website Description Draft

The Lantern Marches are where most Remnants first learn the shape of the broken world. Between Valterra's surviving records and Lyssara's restless roads, the Marches keep a thin line of lanterns burning across old crossings, drowned paths, and settlements that refuse to vanish quietly.
