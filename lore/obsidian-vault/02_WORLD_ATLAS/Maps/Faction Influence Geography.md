# Faction Influence Geography

## Core Rule

Aereth factions are not nations.

They are responses to loss.

Their influence should appear as networks, routes, claims, shrines, archives, outposts, guildhouses, sanctuaries, doctrines, and contested interpretations. Not modern borders. Humanity tried borders already and somehow made paperwork violent. Impressive in the worst way.

## Influence Types

- Strong Influence
- Widespread Presence
- Scattered Outposts
- Disputed / Contested
- Unknown / Forbidden

## Archivists of the Last Echo

- forbidden records
- last memories
- continuity preservation
- registry practices
- post-RE:FRAGMENT truth recovery

Geographic tendencies:

- Western archives
- Valterra record centers
- Hallowmere-adjacent memory sites
- Black Archive contention zones
- ruins with erased histories

## Political Map Tone

A faction map should feel argued over.

Good political atlas details:

- crossed-out labels
- disputed notes
- contradictory marginalia
- faction sigils over the same place
- soft tinting instead of hard borders
- redacted fog areas
- partial erasures

The reader should feel that no power fully understands Aereth, but every power wants to claim they do.
