# Dialogue Samples - Lantern Marches Starter Flow

## Mara Venn, Roadwarden

### First line

> Hold there. If you came by the old road, then either it still remembers you, or it forgot to kill you. Both are worth writing down.

### Send to registry

> Lantern's Rest keeps names before it keeps coin. Go to the Registry Hall. Let Cael mark you in the ledger before the road decides you were never here.

## Archivist Cael Orwyn

### On Remnants

> You are not unusual because you survived. Many survived. You are unusual because the world still answers when your name is spoken.

### On race vs Remnant condition

> Remnant is not your people. It is your condition. Your blood, form, memory, and customs remain your own. The wound simply found you too.

### On RE:FRAGMENT

> Some call it the Erasure. Some the Unmaking. A priest from the south once called it mercy, then forgot his own face. We use RE:FRAGMENT because it is the least dishonest name we have.

## Ilya Marr, Ledger Clerk

### The Bent Road hook

> The third mile marker has appeared five times this week. Same carving. Same crack. Different places. Take a rubbing from it and try not to follow it if it starts following you.

## Echo of Tavin

### Mirrorfen

> I knew this road. I walked it every morning. Then one morning it walked me back.

### Clue

> The ledger did not lose my name. It kept the empty space where my name used to hold the page together.

## Brother Halven, Saint's Crossing

### Cultural ambiguity

> Call it a saint, a stone, a lie, or a comfort. People kneel here because the road is long and grief is heavier than packs.

## Archivist return dialogue

> You saw the pool answer. Good. Bad. Both, usually. Remember this: Fragments do not make you stronger. They make the world less willing to ignore you.
